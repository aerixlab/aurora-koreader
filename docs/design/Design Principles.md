# DESIGN PHILOSOPHY
## Principle 01 — Reading Has a Mood
A good Home screen creates the right mood for reading.

Reading is more than opening a book.

It's a moment of calm.

Aurora is designed to gently transition readers from the busyness of everyday life into a peaceful reading experience.

Every design decision should support that transition.


Calm means...
Plenty of whitespace
Soft colors
No harsh contrast
No unnecessary borders
Gentle typography
Predictable layout
No visual noise

Easy on the eyes means...
Comfortable spacing
Large touch targets
Minimal icons
Soft accent colors
No bright saturated colors
No flashing animations
Clear typography hierarchy
What Aurora should NOT be

I think it's useful to define this too.

Aurora is not:

❌ Busy

❌ Flashy

❌ Over-decorated

❌ "Modern" just for the sake of being modern

❌ Full of gradients and effects

❌ Trying to imitate a phone

------------------

Imagine this.

It's 10:30 PM.

You make tea.

The room is quiet.

Rain outside.

You pick up your Kobo.

When Aurora opens...

It shouldn't feel like you're opening software.

It should feel like...

Walking into your favorite reading corner.

😭

That's Aurora.

---------------------


Aurora should feel...

✓ Calm
✓ Cozy
✓ Quiet
✓ Elegant
✓ Thoughtful
✓ Warm (emotionally, not visually)
✓ Personal
✓ Inviting

Aurora should never feel...

✗ Busy
✗ Loud
✗ Stressful
✗ Gamified
✗ Corporate
✗ Cold
✗ Technical
✗ Overwhelming
